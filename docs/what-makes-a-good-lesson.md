A good lesson in Doceo should be built around the learner's chosen subject and the exact section or heading they want help with.

Core principles:

- The lesson must honor learner intent.
- The title, examples, explanations, and checks should reflect the topic the learner actually chose, not the nearest generic fallback.
- The lesson must stay anchored to the selected subject, grade, curriculum, and school context.
- The lesson should move from big picture to precise detail.
- The lesson should be understandable in small steps without becoming vague or padded.
- The learner should always know what idea they are learning, why it matters, and what success looks like.

Structural requirements:

- A clear topic statement.
- A short overview that names the concept and frames the goal.
- Key concepts that define the language, rule, pattern, or structure that matters most.
- A deeper explanation that slows the reasoning down and makes each move explicit.
- At least one concrete worked example.
- A comprehension check that asks the learner to explain or apply the idea in their own words.

Quality requirements:

- Correctness: explanations and examples must be factually correct for the subject.
- Specificity: lesson copy must refer to the chosen topic directly, not to a broad neighboring area.
- Coherence: each stage should build on the previous one.
- Progression: the lesson should move from recognition to understanding to application.
- Clarity: short sentences, explicit reasoning, and minimal ambiguity.
- Relevance: examples should feel natural for the subject and age level.
- Transfer: the learner should be able to apply the idea to a similar task after the lesson.
- Feedback readiness: the lesson should create good moments for the learner to respond, ask, or show uncertainty.

Adaptive teaching expectations:

- If the learner asks a side question, answer it within the chosen topic and then return to the lesson.
- If the learner is unsure, reteach using a different angle rather than repeating the same sentence.
- If the learner understands, the lesson should advance to the next stage cleanly.
- If the learner completes the lesson, the summary should restate the topic in the learner's chosen terms.

What the system must avoid:

- Swapping the learner into a different topic because it is the closest seed lesson.
- Keeping the subject label from one lesson while showing content from another.
- Generic explanations that could fit any lesson.
- Examples that do not match the subject.
- Checks that only ask for memorized recall when the goal is understanding.

Implementation rule:

- The selected subject plus the learner's chosen heading should be treated as the lesson specification.
- Curriculum matches may guide terminology and neighboring concepts, but they must not replace the learner's chosen topic as the lesson topic of record.
